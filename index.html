IT IS A DOUBLE-ENGED SWORD:

IMPACT ON ENVIRONMENT AND NOT ONLY.

Device How It pollutes?

A Dutch artist visualized environmental pollution from the use of the Internet. To do this, he built a special device that poisons plants with carbon dioxide

Artist from the Netlands Theis Berstecker created a device called MB> CO2, which shows how much CO2 emissions a person produces during the day while using the Internet.

MB> CO2 consists of three monitors, that shows distinctive activities on the Internet: online calls, listening to music, texting, trading NFT, and so on. The monitors are connected to a computer that uses a special algorithm to calculate how many emissions per day the user has made using the Internet. It then emits the calculated amount of carbon dioxide into a spherical terrarium with plants that wither as the CO2 concentration increases. Thus, the device is a visual metaphor of the impact of Internet use on the planet.

But how does IT development, products and technologies can pollute the atmosphere?

The answer is hidden in what makes our devices and gadgets work. The carbon footprint of the Internet depends on the energy needed to power the network, laptops and phones, as well as data centers and servers. According to the International Energy Agency, they account for about 2% of global electricity consumption, which is comparable to the entire aviation industry. By 2040, data storage and processing centers are expected to produce 14% of the world's total CO2 emissions. Moreover, the manufacturing of devices and gadgets harms the environment as well, but it is another large topic to talk about.

"When you type a message, your computer consumes electricity. When you click "Send", it goes to the network, which is also powered by electricity. In the end, the letter ends up somewhere in the cloud, and these data centers consume a huge amount of electricity.

“

We don't think about it because there is no pipe sticking out of the computer from which smoke comes out, but the carbon footprint from IT is huge and continues to grow,

„

said Mike Berners-Lee, brother of Tim Berners-Lee who was the creator of the World Wide Web (WWW).

He added that the main problem lies in the huge number of data centers (data centers) that are located all over the world. They consume so much electricity that efficient communication and information storage capabilities are gradually killing the planet.

One of the ways to reduce CO2 emissions from the IT industry is to build data centers in areas with a cold climate, because 40% of electricity is spent on cooling IT equipment. So, Google built a data storage center in Finland and began to use less energy.

Mr. Frisma, an IT specialist from TUI company gave the interview, where he shared his opinion about how IT impacts the world and environment.

As Mr. Frisma said, another possible way to decrease the amount of carbon dioxide that is released because of our devices is just to start sending one message less during an online conversation. This little thing can bring a lot of difference.

People now have to start thinking about the environment. IT development is integral to modern progress, however, the coin has two sides. We MUST think about how to move forward, not stop our progress, but with the minimum negative impact on our lives and surroundings.

Write at least one message or e-mail less, think about environment!
